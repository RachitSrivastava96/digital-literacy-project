# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

---

## Do's

1. Keep your profile on professional platforms like LinkedIn up to date with your current education, skills, and any projects or achievements worth mentioning.
2. Think before you post — ask yourself whether you would be comfortable with a future employer or professor seeing that content.
3. Use privacy settings to control who can see your personal posts on platforms like Instagram or Facebook.
4. Engage constructively in online discussions — whether on LinkedIn, Reddit, or Stack Overflow — by adding value and staying respectful.
5. Use your college email for signing up on academic and professional platforms to keep your digital identity organised and credible.

---

## Don'ts

1. Do not share personal information like your phone number, home address, or daily routine publicly on any social media platform.
2. Do not post or share content that is offensive, discriminatory, or politically inflammatory — even as a joke. It can have real consequences on your academic and professional reputation.
3. Do not use the same password across multiple platforms. A single breach can compromise all your accounts.
4. Do not engage with or share unverified news, rumours, or forwarded messages without checking the source first.
5. Do not publicly vent about your college, professors, or classmates online — what feels like a private rant can spread quickly and reflect poorly on you.
