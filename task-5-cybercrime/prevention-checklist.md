# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

A practical guide for college students in India to protect their digital identity and stay safe online.

---

## General Digital Safety

1. Use a strong, unique password for every account. A good password is at least 12 characters long and combines letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms.

2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts — your email, social media, and any platform linked to your bank or college portal.

3. Never click on links in emails or SMS messages that ask you to verify your account, claim a prize, or warn of a suspension. Always go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.

4. Before uploading your Aadhaar, PAN, or any government ID to any website, verify that the platform is official and legitimate. Avoid submitting documents to third-party scholarship or job portals that are not well known.

5. Regularly check your email and phone for unfamiliar login alerts or account activity. If you receive an OTP you did not request, treat it as a warning sign and change your password immediately.

6. Avoid using public Wi-Fi networks (in cafes, malls, or bus stands) to access your bank account, college portal, or any platform where you are logged in.

## UPI and Financial Safety

7. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or card CVV with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank's customer support. Banks never ask for these details over a call or message.

8. Be cautious of "receive money" requests on UPI apps. Scammers often send collect requests with messages like "tap to receive your refund" — entering your PIN on a collect request sends money out, it does not bring money in.

---

## If Something Goes Wrong

- Report cybercrime immediately at the National Cyber Crime Portal: **cybercrime.gov.in**
- Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930** (available 24x7)
- Also inform your bank immediately if your financial accounts are involved — most banks have a fraud reporting line that can freeze transactions quickly.
